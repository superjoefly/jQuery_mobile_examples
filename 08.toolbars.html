jQuery Mobile Toolbars

Toolbar elements are often placed inside headers and footers - for "easy access" navigation:

Header Bars

The header is located at the top of the page and usually contains a page title/logo, or one or two buttons (typically home, options or search).

You can add buttons to the left and/or right side of the header.

Note: a header can contain one or two buttons, while a footer has no limit.

Footer Bars

The footer is located at the bottom of the page.

The footer is more flexible than the header - it is more functional and changeable throughout pages and can therefore contain as many buttons as needed.

Example: footer with buttons

Note: the buttons in the footer are not centered by default...to fix this use CSS:

You can also group buttons in the footer horizontally or vertically:

Positioning Headers and Footers

The header and footer can be positioned in three ways:

  • Inline - default; header and footer are inline with page content.
  • Fixed - headers and footers will remain positioned at the top and bottom of the page.
  • Fullscreen - behaves like fixed; headers and footers will remain positioned at the top and bottom, but also over the page content. It is slightly see through.

Use the data-position attribute to position your headers and footers.

Tip: the fullscreen position is ideal for photos, images and video.

Tip: tapping the screen will hide and show footers and headers for both fixed and fullscreen positions.
